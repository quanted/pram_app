  • Models for Pesticide Risk Assessment

    When EPA assesses the risk of a pesticide to the environment, it considers the toxicity of the pesticide as well as the amount of pesticide to which the environment may be exposed. In assessing exposure, scientists frequently use mathematical models to predict pesticide concentrations in soil, sediment, air, food and water.

  • Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act

    FIFRA regulates pesticide distribution, sale and use. Before EPA may register a pesticide under FIFRA, the applicant must show, among other things, that using the pesticide according to specifications 'will not generally cause unreasonable adverse effects on the environment.'

  • Registration Review

    EPA reviews each registered pesticide at least every 15 years to determine whether it continues to meet the FIFRA standard for registration.

  • Endangered Species

    EPA evaluates extensive environmental fate and toxicity data to determine how a pesticide will move through and break down in the environment and whether potential exposure to the pesticide will result in adverse effects to wildlife and vegetation to determine whether a pesticide may be licensed for use.
